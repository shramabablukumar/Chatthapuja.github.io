Chattha Puja - A Celebration of Gratitude

Chattha Puja is a revered Hindu festival celebrated primarily in the states of Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, Jharkhand, and Nepal, paying homage to the Sun God, Surya. It is a festival of deep devotion and gratitude for the Sun's vital role in sustaining life on Earth. The festival is celebrated six days after Diwali, typically falling in October or November. During this time, devotees express their heartfelt thanks to the Sun God for the blessings of health, prosperity, and happiness.

The celebration of Chattha Puja is marked by fasting and purification rituals. Devotees engage in a rigorous fast, dedicating themselves to Surya and his consort Usha, seeking their blessings. The main event of the puja occurs during sunrise and sunset on the sixth day of the festival, when devotees offer "arghya" or ritualistic water offerings to the Sun. Families gather near rivers, ponds, or any water body to perform the sacred rituals, creating an atmosphere of devotion and spiritual purity.
